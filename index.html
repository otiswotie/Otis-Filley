Environmental Investigation

More than a Fish Kill

More than a Fish Kill explores how artists, fisheries managers and First Nations custodians came together in the aftermath of devastating fish kills along the Barka/Baaka (Darling River) and turned these ecological disasters into catalysts for cultural connection and revival.

National Museum of Australia | 2024 Writer, Director, Editor

The film tells the story of a remarkable collaboration that interweaves art, science and ancient knowledge to care for communities, honour our rivers and reshape how we live, now and into the future. The film will be made accessible to the public later in the year.

Mutawintji

2022-2024 | Mutawintji (NSW)

Mutawintji National Park is celebrated for its profound cultural and historical importance, alongside its captivating desert vistas and indigenous heritage. Recent events include a commemorative celebration marking 40 years since the significant land handback, a visit from U.S. Park Service director Charles F. Sams III to explore co-management strategies, and a Corroboree echoing the last traditional gathering held on the site a century ago.

Environmental Documentation

Menindee Floods

2022 | Menindee (NSW)

In late 2022, Menindee experienced flooding as part of the ongoing pattern of changes in the Darling-Baaka River. These fluctuations underscore the difficulties faced by river-dependent communities as they navigate a constantly changing environment, often influenced more by political interests and human intervention than by the river's natural flow patterns.

The Guardian | 2022 Journalist • Photography

"What comes, comes": Menindee residents stoic in face of looming flood peak - Locals prepare for isolation as record water levels predicted to inundate the far-west NSW community.

Documentary • The Guardian

Trampling Victoria's Alps: how brumbies are destroying the native habitat

The Guardian 2024
Videographer, Journalist, Editor

At Native Cat Flat in Victoria's Alpine national park, four fenced-off areas show a strikingly different ecology, highlighting the damage wrought by more than 2,700 feral horses in the area. Behind the fences, lush sphagnum, dense vegetation, grass tussocks, shrubs and herbs thrive.

Documentary Series

The Cod and the Economy

This Is Water | Episode 2

Video Production • Editing
Collaborative production with Dan Schulz

In January 2019, Menindee faced an ecological crisis when hundreds of thousands of fish, including the iconic Murray Cod, died in the Darling-Baaka River. This episode follows local volunteers as they rescue surviving fish under the guidance of NSW Department of Primary Industries. Dr. Paul Sinclair highlights the Murray Cod's role in connecting people to the river and how financial systems prioritize irrigation at the expense of river health.

2020 Video Production • Editing

The episode explores how the fish kill galvanized community action, underscoring the urgent need to address the ecological imbalance in the river system.

Cultural Politics

Art Legacy of Broken Hill

2022-2023 | Broken Hill (NSW)

The curator, gallery manager and two other senior staff resign from Broken Hill's art gallery as the council exerts its influence over what constitutes 'local' art. A $35,000 rebranding effort to elevate the gallery's market position was abandoned by Council in favor of reverting to its 2002 identity.

Community Engagement

Youth Ideas Lab

ABC Heywire | 2021 | Broken Hill (NSW)

ABC Heywire is a program that highlights the perspectives of young people in rural and regional areas of Australia. Otis collaborated with the ABC team and students of Broken Hill High School to produce films for the 2021 Heywire event, which was exclusively held in Broken Hill.

Australian Broadcasting Corporation | 2021 Video • Editing

The ideas developed at the Heywire Youth Ideas Lab were backed by $150,000 in grants from the Foundation for Rural & Regional Renewal, encouraging young Australians to share their stories and ideas for making positive changes in their communities.
