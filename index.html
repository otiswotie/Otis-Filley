Trampling Victoria's Alps

How brumbies are destroying the native habitat

The Guardian 2024
Videographer, Journalist, Editor

An investigation into the environmental impact of wild horses in Victoria's Alpine National Park, examining the tension between cultural heritage and ecological conservation. This documentary piece reveals the complex debate surrounding brumby management and its implications for native ecosystems.

Working with ecologists, Traditional Owners, and park managers, this investigation documents the complex environmental and cultural issues surrounding wild horse management in Australia's alpine regions. The story examines scientific evidence of environmental damage while exploring the cultural significance these animals hold for some communities.

Environmental Policy

Water Rights and Regional Communities

An ongoing investigation into water allocation policies and their impact on rural communities across eastern Australia. This work examines how water trading markets, irrigation infrastructure, and climate variability shape the lives of farmers, Indigenous communities, and small towns.

2022–Present Journalism • Photography • Data Analysis

Through embedded reporting in rural communities, this project traces the human stories behind water policy debates. From cotton farmers in Queensland to rice growers in the Riverina, the work examines how abstract policy decisions translate into lived experiences of uncertainty, adaptation, and resilience.

Video Production • Editing

Art Installation • FELTspace

Tending Toxicity

FELTspace Adelaide 2022
Film by Otis Filley and Dan Schulz
Sound design by Damien Sutton

A multimedia installation exploring environmental contamination and community response through immersive media. The work examines how communities adapt to and resist environmental degradation, using the Menindee Lake system as a case study.

Presented as a three-channel video installation with spatial audio, "Tending Toxicity" invites viewers to experience the environmental and social complexity of the Menindee Lakes crisis. The work combines documentary footage, soundscapes, and archival material to create an immersive exploration of environmental contamination and community resilience.

Environmental Restoration

Restoring the Rivers

Following ecological restoration projects across the Murray-Darling Basin, this ongoing series documents efforts to rehabilitate damaged river systems and restore native fish populations, wetland habitats, and floodplain ecosystems.

2023–Present Photography • Science Communication • Long-term Documentation

Working with scientists, land managers, and local communities, this project tracks the slow but essential work of environmental restoration. From fish ladders and wetland reconstruction to revegetation programs, the work documents both the challenges and successes of ecosystem recovery.
