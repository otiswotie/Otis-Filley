Environmental Investigation

More than a Fish Kill

A multi-year investigation into the Murray-Darling Basin crisis, documenting the intersection of ecological collapse, policy failure, and community impact across Australia's most significant river system.

2019–2023 Photography • Video • Journalism

This ongoing investigation spans multiple years of research, community engagement, and environmental monitoring across the Murray-Darling Basin. From mass fish kills to water allocation disputes, the work examines how environmental degradation intersects with economic policy and community resilience.

Social Documentary

Fighting to Save Public Housing

Documenting community resistance and the human impact of urban development pressures on Melbourne's public housing estates. A story of displacement, community solidarity, and the fight for housing justice.

2023–2024 Photography • Journalism

As gentrification pressures mount across Melbourne, public housing residents face increasing uncertainty. This documentary project follows community organizers, residents, and advocates as they navigate policy changes and development pressures.

Environmental Policy

Water Rights and Regional Communities

An ongoing investigation into water allocation policies and their impact on rural communities across eastern Australia. This work examines how water trading markets, irrigation infrastructure, and climate variability shape the lives of farmers and small towns.

2022–Present Journalism • Photography • Data Analysis

Through embedded reporting in rural communities, this project traces the human stories behind water policy debates. From cotton farmers in Queensland to rice growers in the Riverina, the work examines how abstract policy decisions translate into lived experiences.

Climate Adaptation

Small Towns, Big Changes

A long-term documentation project following how small Australian towns adapt to changing climate conditions. From drought-affected communities to coastal towns facing sea-level rise, this work examines local responses to global environmental change.

2020–Present Photography • Video • Community Stories

Through embedded reporting in regional communities, this project documents the innovative ways small towns are adapting to environmental change. From water conservation initiatives to economic diversification strategies, the work reveals how local knowledge drives practical responses to climate challenges.

Indigenous Rights • Environmental Justice

Country and Water Rights

Documenting Traditional Owner perspectives on water management and environmental policy across the Murray-Darling Basin. This collaborative project examines how Indigenous communities assert their rights while navigating complex negotiations with government and industry.

2021–Present Photography • Journalism • Community Collaboration

Working closely with Traditional Owner groups, this project documents the intersection of cultural obligations, environmental concerns, and economic pressures in contemporary water management debates.

Environmental Restoration

Restoring the Rivers

Following ecological restoration projects across the Murray-Darling Basin, this ongoing series documents efforts to rehabilitate damaged river systems and restore native fish populations and wetland habitats.

2023–Present Photography • Science Communication

Working with scientists, land managers, and local communities, this project tracks the slow but essential work of environmental restoration. From fish ladders to wetland reconstruction, the work documents both challenges and successes of ecosystem recovery.

Sustainable Agriculture

Beyond Industrial Farming

Following farmers transitioning to regenerative agriculture practices across eastern Australia. This project examines how sustainable land management techniques can restore soil health, increase biodiversity, and build climate resilience.

2023–Present Photography • Agriculture • Environmental Science

From carbon farming initiatives to rotational grazing systems, this work documents how progressive farmers are implementing practices that restore ecological function while maintaining economic viability.
